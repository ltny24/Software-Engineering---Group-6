## 2. Communication Plan

### 2.1 Communication Tools
The team mainly communicates through **Messenger** for announcements, discussions, and daily coordination. Online meetings are conducted via **Google Meet** when needed.

### 2.2 Meeting Schedule
Each sprint includes:
  - 1 Sprint Planning meeting
  - 2 Scrum meetings
  - 1 Sprint Review/Retrospective meeting

The team will conduct 1–2 meetings per week. Each meeting normally lasts around 1 to 1.5 hours.
Meetings may be held online or offline depending on members’ availability.


### 2.3 Communication and Attendance Rules

Members are expected to respond or acknowledge tagged messages within 24 hours to confirm that they have read the notification.

If a member cannot attend a meeting, they must notify the team beforehand so meeting summaries and assigned tasks can be shared afterward. Missing meetings or failing to respond to important notifications without prior notice may affect internal performance evaluations.

## 3. Work Schedule and Deadlines

### 3.1 Sprint Structure

The project timeline follows the PA sprint structure of the course:
- PA1 = Sprint 1
- PA2 = Sprint 2
- PA3 = Sprint 3
- PA4 = Sprint 4
- PA5 = Sprint 5

Each sprint includes Sprint Planning, Scrum meetings, implementation tasks, and a Sprint Review/Retrospective session.

### 3.2 Task Assignment and Deadline Rules

Tasks are assigned during Sprint Planning meetings based on members’ strengths, interests, workload, and learning goals.

Every task created must include: A clear task owner, a creation date, a deadline and a completion status.

All tasks must be created and assigned before work begins. Retroactively creating tasks or marking completed work afterward is not allowed.

If a member expects delays due to blockers, exams, or personal workload, they must notify the Team Leader and Project Manager at least 48 hours in advance so the team can adjust schedules or redistribute tasks if necessary.

## 4. Code and Documentation Standards

The project follows consistent coding and formatting conventions across the entire codebase. Variable names, comments, commit messages, and API endpoints are written in English to maintain readability and collaboration consistency. 

**Repository Structure:**

- `/src`: source code

- `/docs`: documentation
  - `management/`: planning documents, reports (weekly report, project status report, etc.)
  - `requirements/`: vision document, use cases
  - `analysis-and-design/`: software architecture document, diagrams, UI design
  - `test/`: test plan, test cases, test reports

All reports and documentation are written in Markdown (`.md`). Mermaid syntax is used for diagrams whenever possible. 

Sensitive information such as API keys or credentials must not be committed to the repository.

## 5. Accountability and Performance

### 5.1 Evaluation Criteria
Contributions are evaluated through:
  - Jira task completion
  - Commit history
  - Participation in meetings and discussions
  - Quality of assigned work

All members are expected to actively contribute to both technical and collaborative activities throughout the project.

### 5.2 Handling Underperformance
If a member experiences difficulties with assigned tasks or deadlines, the issue should be communicated early so the team can provide support or adjust responsibilities when necessary. 

In cases where a member repeatedly misses deadlines or shows a lack of participation without a reasonable explanation, the team will first discuss the issue privately to identify possible causes and solutions. If the situation continues, tasks may be redistributed and the member’s contribution level may be reflected in the final project evaluation.

### 5.3 Contribution Evaluation Scale

Member contributions may be internally evaluated using the following scale:

- **10:** Excellent contribution, completes tasks on time with high quality, actively supports other members, and shows strong responsibility and initiative.
- **8–9:** Completes assigned tasks on time with good quality and actively participates in team activities.
- **6–7:** Completes assigned tasks with acceptable results but may occasionally violate team rules or require reminders.
- **1–5:** Frequently misses deadlines, contributes minimally, or shows limited collaboration with the team.
- **0:** Removed from the team or no longer participates in the project.

Contribution evaluations may also be reflected through internal percentage distributions discussed by the team when necessary.

## 6. Decision-Making Process

### 6.1 Team Discussion and Consensus
The team prioritizes open discussion and collaboration when making technical, design, or project management decisions. Members are encouraged to share ideas and discuss alternative solutions before reaching a final conclusion.

### 6.2 Voting and Final Decisions
Whenever possible, decisions should be made through team consensus. If different opinions remain after discussion, the issue will be resolved through majority voting, where each member has equal voting rights. 

In urgent situations that require immediate action to avoid delaying project progress, the Team Leader holds the responsibility of making the final decision after considering the team’s opinions.

---

## 7. Conflict Resolution

### 7.1 Internal Discussion 
Members are encouraged to resolve disagreements professionally through respectful communication and direct discussion. Conflicts related to workload, implementation approaches, scheduling, or collaboration should first be discussed between the involved members. 

### 7.2 Mediation and Escalation 
If the issue cannot be resolved directly, it will be discussed during a team meeting so other members can help mediate the situation. In serious cases that significantly affect teamwork or project progress, the team may request support from the Teaching Assistant (TA) or Instructor.

---

## 8. Review and Update Process

### 8.1 Contract Review
This contract will be reviewed at the end of each sprint during the Sprint Review or Retrospective meeting to ensure that it still matches the team’s workflow and collaboration process.

### 8.2 Amendments
Any member may suggest updates or improvements to the contract during the project. Proposed changes will be discussed openly and applied after receiving approval from the majority of the team.
